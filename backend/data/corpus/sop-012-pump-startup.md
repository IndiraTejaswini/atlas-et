# SOP-012 — Crude Charge Pump Startup & Changeover

Applies to: P-101A and P-101B. Governing standards: OISD-STD-105 (work permit where applicable), OEM manual OEM-SLZ-OHH.

## Pre-Start Checks
1. Confirm suction valve fully open, discharge valve closed.
2. Verify Plan 32 seal flush is in service: MOV-118 open, flush flow visible on FI, flush pressure at least 1.5 barg above seal chamber.
3. Check bearing oil level at sight glass centre (ISO VG 68).
4. Confirm minimum-flow line open.
5. Vent casing until liquid-full; crude charge pumps must never be run dry.

## Startup
6. Start motor; observe discharge pressure rise to approx 42 barg.
7. Slowly open discharge valve while monitoring motor amps.
8. Verify vibration below 7.1 mm/s; if above, stop and notify rotating equipment engineer.

## Changeover (A to B or B to A)
9. Start standby pump per steps 1-8 with discharge cracked open.
10. Gradually swap load between pumps; never leave both discharge valves pinched.
11. After changeover, keep the stopped pump's seal flush IN SERVICE for 30 minutes, then isolate.

**Caution:** Do not bypass the low suction pressure interlock I-301. Bypassing caused cavitation damage in past operation.

Note (Rev 3): Step 2 strengthened after near-miss INC-451 — flush verification is now a hold point requiring shift supervisor sign-off.
