# Handover Notes — R. Sharma (retiring January 2026)

Informal notes for A. Verma and the Unit 300 team. Things that are in my head and not in any manual.

## Crude charge pumps P-101A/B
- The Plan 32 flush line is the weak point of these pumps. It is only 1/2 inch and it WILL wax up whenever we run Mangala or any high-wax slate, especially November to February when ambient drops. The 2024 seal failures (WO-2301, WO-2415) were both this. Watch the flush flow indicator daily in winter, not just at PM intervals.
- Trick: if flush flow looks sluggish, a 10-minute hot diesel backflush through MOV-118 clears early wax before it hardens. We never wrote this into a procedure — it should go into SOP-012.
- Never trust the standby pump. Wax forms in the stagnant B-pump flush line even faster than the running pump. Rotate A/B monthly.

## E-104 preheat exchanger
- After the slate change, fouling roughly doubled (see WO-2734). Plan cleaning every 18 months, not 3 years, or furnace duty will quietly eat the margin.
- The south saddle area traps rainwater under insulation — this is the third time CUI has shown up there since 1998 (see INSP-088). When they reinsulate, insist on the drainage detail from the 2005 repair; the drawing note was lost in a revision.

## Column C-201
- Tray 14 downcomer has a history of salt deposition when overhead wash rate is cut below 8 m3/hr. If column dP creeps up in week 2-3 after a wash cut, that is the reason — restore wash before considering a shutdown.

## General
- Most of what goes wrong here has gone wrong before. Check the old work orders and incident files before starting any RCA — the answer is usually already written down somewhere.
