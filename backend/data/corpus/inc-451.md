# Incident Report INC-451 — Near-Miss: Vapor Release at P-101A

**Event:** At 14:20, field operator noticed hydrocarbon vapor plume from the seal area of running pump P-101A. Area cordoned, unit rate reduced, standby pump P-101B brought in per SOP-012. No ignition, no injury. Gas detector at grade peaked at 22% LEL.

**Immediate cause:** Mechanical seal face failure releasing crude vapor. Pump repaired under WO-2415.

**Root cause analysis (5-Why):**
1. Seal faces overheated → 2. Plan 32 flush flow degraded → 3. Flush line waxed up → 4. Crude slate changed to waxy Mangala crude in Jan 2024 without reviewing seal support systems → 5. **Management of Change for the crude slate change did not include a rotating-equipment review.**

**Contributing factor:** Operator rounds checklist did not include flush flow verification; the check existed only in the OEM manual, not in SOP-012 (Rev 2 at the time).

**Actions:**
1. SOP-012 revised to Rev 3 making flush verification a sign-off hold point — DONE (2024-09).
2. Monthly flush strainer cleaning added to PM routes — DONE.
3. MOC procedure updated: crude slate changes require rotating equipment review — DONE.
4. Evaluate heat tracing for Plan 32 lines before winter — OPEN, owner A. Verma.

**Learning:** A feedstock change is an equipment change. Slate changes must trigger review of seal support, wash oil and tracing systems. Similar wax-blockage pattern later found on P-101B (WO-2688).

**Investigated by:** P. Nair (Safety Officer), A. Verma, R. Sharma.
